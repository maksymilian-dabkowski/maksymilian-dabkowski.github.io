A'ingae

Research on A'ingae (or Cofán, ISO 639-3: con), an Amazonian language isolate of northeast Ecuador and southern Colombia.

Phonology

My research focuses on the morphophonology of A'ingae verbal stress. I provide the most detailed description of A'ingae's rich inflectional paradigm to date, a novel description of its morphologically-conditioned stress assignment, and a formal account of the complex data. I argue that A'ingae has a typologically unatested type of glottal accent.

Semantics

I describe the syntax and semantics of A'ingae's apprehensional domain in collaboration with Prof. Scott AnderBois. We are currently working on a formalization of the apprehensional semantics, explaining the typological relation among various apprehensional morphemes and their meanings.

The apprehensional domain in A’ingae, as first author, with Scott AnderBois. In an untitled volume on apprehensive/precautioning markers, Studies in Diversity Linguistics. Language Science Press. In press.  Chapter  Abstract

Documentation

I have carried out community-engaged language documentation in the Ecuadorian communities of A'ingae speakers. I collected over 14 hours of interviews in A'ingae and trained two research assistants to record, transcribe, and translate the collected material. I analyze language data, archive language materials, and develop new lines of theoretical inquiry. Lastly, I collaborate on developing and implementing LingView, a web interface for viewing translated and analyzed multilingual audio and video recordings accessible to the Cofán people.

not A'ingae

Other research and peri-academic miscellanea.

Yucatec Maya syntax

My syntactic research focused on Yucatec Maya. I formalized a fragment of the language's grammar and argued against a nominalization analysis of the language's intransitive subjunctive control.

Philosophy of language

I argue against Williamson (1994)’s epistemic theory of vagueness, and propose that a Wittgensteinian picture of language (1953) better accommodates vague predicates.

Processing of entailment

I designed and implemented an experimental paradigm investigating the nature of logical reasoning in language processing tasks. The collaborative study of mental representations of formal notions such as entailment and polarity work has been carried out with Brown Languaguage and Though Lab under the supervision of Prof. Roman Feiman.
